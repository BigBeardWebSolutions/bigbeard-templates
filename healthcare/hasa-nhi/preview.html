Understanding NHI

Frequently Asked Questions

Get clear, evidence-based answers to the most common questions about National Health Insurance and what it means for you.

The NHI is a health financing system designed to pool funds to provide universal access to quality, affordable personal health services for all South Africans based on their health needs, irrespective of their socio-economic status. It aims to ensure that the use of health services does not result in financial hardship for individuals and their families.

The NHI Fund will be financed through a combination of general tax revenue, a payroll tax on employers, a surcharge on personal income tax, and reallocation of medical tax credits. The exact contribution rates and fiscal mechanisms are still under consultation and will be phased in over the implementation period.

Under the NHI Bill, medical schemes will only be allowed to offer complementary cover for services not reimbursed by the NHI Fund. This means that medical schemes will not be able to duplicate services that are already covered under the NHI. The transition will be phased in over time as the NHI system becomes operational.

The NHI is being implemented in a phased approach. The current phase focuses on strengthening the public health system and piloting key components. Full implementation, including the establishment of the NHI Fund and contracting of healthcare providers, is expected to take several more years as infrastructure, systems, and workforce capacity are built up.
